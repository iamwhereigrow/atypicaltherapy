Privacy Practices & Legal Notice

Atypical Therapy (DBA for iamwhereigrow, LLC)

Effective Date of This Notice: March 15, 2025

Website: www.atypicaltherapy.life

I. My Legal Duty to Protect Your Health Information

As a licensed mental health professional, I am legally required to maintain the privacy and security of your protected health information (PHI), as defined under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), codified at 45 CFR §§ 164.500–534.

This notice outlines how your PHI may be used and disclosed and describes your legal rights. I am required by law to:

  • Maintain the privacy of your PHI
  • Provide you with this Notice of my legal duties and privacy practices
  • Comply with the terms of this Notice currently in effect

I reserve the right to revise this Notice at any time. Changes will apply to all PHI I maintain. The updated Notice will be available upon request and on the website.

II. Uses and Disclosures Without Written Authorization

For Treatment, Payment, and Health Care Operations: Your PHI may be used or disclosed without your written authorization for treatment coordination, insurance claims, billing, and administration. Disclosures for treatment purposes may exceed the “minimum necessary” standard.

Additional Permitted Disclosures Include:

  • When required by law
  • Public health and safety concerns
  • Judicial, administrative, or law enforcement requests
  • Health oversight audits
  • To coroners or for research (where approved)
  • Workers’ compensation and government functions
  • Appointment reminders or alternative treatment options

III. Uses and Disclosures That Require Authorization

Your explicit written consent is required for:

  • Use of psychotherapy notes (with exceptions)
  • Marketing communications
  • Sale of PHI

You may revoke your authorization in writing at any time unless already acted upon.

IV. Disclosures You May Object To

Unless you object, I may share PHI with people involved in your care or payment. This includes appointment reminders and care updates. You may revoke this permission at any time.

V. Your Rights Under HIPAA

You have the right to:

  • Request restrictions on PHI disclosures
  • Request confidential communications
  • Access and copy your clinical record (excluding psychotherapy notes)
  • Request corrections to incomplete/inaccurate records
  • Receive an accounting of certain disclosures
  • Request a paper or electronic copy of this Notice

All requests must be made in writing.

VI. My Responsibilities

  • Maintain privacy and security of your PHI
  • Inform you of data breaches involving your PHI
  • Comply with the Notice and its terms
  • Not use/share PHI without your consent, unless legally permitted

VII. Crisis Support and Scope of Practice

Emergency Resources: Atypical Therapy does not provide 24/7 crisis intervention. In an emergency, contact:

  • 911
  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
  • The Trevor Project: 1-866-488-7386
  • Your nearest emergency department

Emails, text, and client portal messages are not monitored continuously.

Scope: This practice offers outpatient psychotherapy. It does not provide medication or diagnostic medical care. Clients needing medication should consult a licensed physician.

VIII. Communication Policy

Communications may occur via secure portal, encrypted email, or HIPAA-compliant text. These channels are not monitored 24/7. Please allow up to two business days for non-urgent responses.

IX. Good Faith Estimate

Under the No Surprises Act, uninsured or self-pay clients are entitled to a Good Faith Estimate of expected service costs. This estimate will be provided prior to the first session, upon request, or when services change significantly. Visit cms.gov/nosurprises for more details.

X. Complaints and Contact Information

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with:

  • U.S. Dept. of Health and Human Services – Office for Civil Rights
  • Phone: 1-877-696-6775
  • Website: www.hhs.gov/ocr

Complaints may also be submitted directly to Atypical Therapy. No retaliation will occur.

XI. Acknowledgment

By accessing the website or services of Atypical Therapy, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the terms of this Notice.
